This book examines the worldwide expansion of theme parks and frames them as
territorial devices that reshape land use, urban hierarchies, and destination
positioning strategies.

Its key contribution is a multiscalar approach linking leisure industries,
capital investment, and local governance. It remains highly relevant for
research on tourism geography and urban renewal policies.
