This volume provides a broad view of Europe by integrating physical geography,
demographic change, economic organization, and territorial policy. Its main
strength is the way it moves from regional description to cross-regional
comparison.

For teaching and applied research, it remains a strong reference for courses in
regional geography and spatial planning, especially when connecting European,
national, and local scales.
